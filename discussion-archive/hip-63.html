John Mac 09/05/2022 11:31 AM
I'd like there to be a system in place, specifically for 5G (and all other future subSAOs). With 5G, if someone meets their criteria to receive MOBILE (passing heartbeats), proves via irrefutable evidence that they were passing heartbeats and should have received MOBILE- then there need to be a system in place for who is responsible to determine if they should've received MOBILE and who is responsible to compensate said mobile. IMO this should be the responsibility of the foundation, who have received 50 billion MOBILE tokens. The issue becomes, "hey, that's a lot of work! Why should we be responsible?" First off, with mobile/5G, it relatively isn't a lot of work. There's only a few thousand 5G operators, not nearly a million like LoRa. Additionally, perhaps, we can incentivize foundation individuals to do the work, by offering 10% of any distributed mobile to the foundation member that does the work (obviously only in these specific occurrences where someone was passing heartbeats and should have been rewarded but was not). For example, in my case where I went a total of 3 days (thus far) passing heartbeats and not receiving mobile, I'm owed around 130,000 MOBILE. Thus, the foundation member that would verify and approve my 130,000 mobile claim, would receive 10% or 13,000 mobile out of my 130,000 (leaving me with 117,000 mobile of the 130,000 lost and the Foundation member that did the work with 10% or 13,000 MOBILE for doing the work).
