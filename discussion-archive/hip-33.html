ChiefHelium 07/22/2021 7:49 PM
I would vote one world one network. European citizens should take up their limiting radio laws with their government, citing Helium's benefits (and the benefits to Helium afforded under foreign rules) as a reason to ease restrictions on their radio spectrum. To make a point by taking it to an extreme macrocosm, let's say a dictatorship country outlaws all unlicensed radio entirely. Shall we all send its citizens HNT just because their government has suppressed their ability to spread the network? And by microcosm, what if a local government or housing association demands a fee for allowing an antenna be installed on someone's roof - shall we all bear that burden equally as well? Or, the only scale upon which this notion is reasonable is in between, that is, continental/regional? I disagree that Helium ought accommodate petitions from those who are better served by petitioning the source of the problem - that their own government ought grant freedoms to more fully and readily participate - both in mild and extreme cases of government restrictions. That is what is actually in the best interest of the network. That is how our common interest ought be aligned, worldwide. All else equal, I'd rather donate HNT to a legal fund to support that cause, than accept reduced earnings out of some sense of fairness but with zero incentive to genuinely improve the situation in these regions.
