# Advisory Council: Roles, Responsibilities, and Access

*The Governance Framework for the HIP-149 Advisory Council.*

## What This Document Covers

This document is the **Governance Framework** for the Advisory Council established under [HIP 149][hip149]. It describes what the Council does, its mandate and authority, what Council members can and cannot access, the confidentiality and trading obligations that come with the seat, how members are compensated, and how removal works.

It is an appendix to and extension of HIP 149, not an agreement with the Receiving Entity. "Receiving Entity" means the entity receiving the operations and growth supplement (the "Supplement") authorized by [HIP 149 Decision 2][hip149-d2]; it is currently Nova Labs, Inc. ("Nova"). Two things have binding force, through two different paths:

- **The Council's authority and the Supplement parameters** are established by HIP 149 and this Governance Framework, and are amendable only by community vote (and, for the Supplement, a program upgrade). This document is community-owned governance; it is not created or amended through a contract with the Receiving Entity.
- **The member-level operational terms** (the obligations each member takes on once seated) take binding effect through the [Confidentiality and Participation Agreement (CPA)][council-cpa] each member signs with the Receiving Entity. The CPA references this Governance Framework for context; this document does not by itself create obligations enforceable between the Receiving Entity and a member.

A later community amendment to this document updates its text going forward but does not, by itself, change a signed CPA. Where a binding member obligation in a signed CPA differs from this document, the CPA as executed governs for that member.

## Purpose

[HIP 149 Decision 2][hip149-d2] authorizes new HNT to be minted per-epoch into a vault administered by the Receiving Entity, across two self-terminating windows; see that decision for the mint rate, window durations, and boundary timestamps. The Council exists to oversee the allocation and use of the Supplement and to monitor progress toward the goals tied to the mint:

1. Long-term financial viability of the entity managing the network, with the target of becoming cash-flow neutral within the three-year mint period.
2. Continued expansion of the Helium Network, with increased burn of HNT in a manner that is financially sustainable over the long term.

The Council is mandated to act in the best interests of the Helium Network and its HNT holders by ensuring Supplement funds are used effectively to advance these goals. Its oversight authority is limited to matters materially related to the Supplement: its use, allocation, monetization, DC burn alignment, market impact, and progress toward the stated goals.

The Receiving Entity's good-faith commitments on monetization, the non-compete, DC burn alignment, and long-term alignment are set out in the Receiving Entity Agreement in [HIP 149][hip149]. The Council oversees conformance with those commitments and may escalate a curtailment if it concludes they are not being honored; it does not approve, deny, or direct individual transactions.

The Council is an oversight body. It is not a board of directors, a management committee, or an executive team. Council members do not manage the Receiving Entity, do not direct its business operations, and do not have authority over its day-to-day decisions.

## Composition

Canonical: [HIP 149 Decision 4][hip149-d4]. In summary:

- **5 community-nominated seats.** Any veHNT (vote-escrowed HNT) holder above a low nomination threshold may self-nominate or nominate others, with the nominee's consent. Each holder may vote for up to five candidates; nominees are confirmed by veHNT-weighted vote. The Receiving Entity recuses the veHNT positions it directly controls from voting on these five seats; it does not control, and makes no commitment for, the voting of its individual equity holders.
- **2 Receiving-Entity-appointed seats.** Designated by the Receiving Entity; voting members; the Receiving Entity is responsible for their conduct and may replace them at any time at its discretion. They serve unpaid.
- **All members' identities are disclosed to the Helium community.** No member may serve anonymously.

## Term

All seven seats run with the Supplement. The Council's term ends on the earliest of: a community vote to dissolve the Council; full distribution of the ~141M HNT Supplement; or the Receiving Entity declining further distributions. If the Supplement is suspended early through a curtailment, the Council survives 90 days to facilitate restart discussions, then dissolves unless a restart HIP is actively in the voting process. There are no automatic renewals.

## Authority

Canonical: [HIP 149 Decision 4][hip149-d4]. The Council's authority is limited to oversight of the Supplement. All actions must be exercised in connection with evaluating whether Supplement spending and the Receiving Entity's conduct are consistent with HIP 149's stated goals. The authority does not extend to general oversight of the Receiving Entity's business, and any demand for disclosure must be reasonably related to the Supplement.

**Standard actions** (3 days' notice; quorum 5 of 7 seated, including at least 3 community seats; simple majority):

- Demand disclosure from the Receiving Entity regarding use of Supplement funds and progress toward the stated goals.
- Publish dissent on the Receiving Entity's use of Supplement funds.
- Recommend course-correction on Supplement spending.

**Escalation action** (5 days' notice and a 5-day Council voting period; quorum 5 of 7 seated, including at least 3 community seats; simple majority):

- Recommend a curtailment of the Supplement (a reduction, pause, or halt). The Receiving Entity's 2 seats alone cannot trigger or block.

**Curtailment process.** A curtailment recommendation does not take effect by itself and creates no unilateral on-chain lever. Following the Council vote it triggers a community vote, announced within 3 days, with a minimum 7-day discussion period and a 7-day voting period, approved by a simple majority of voted veHNT. On approval, the curtailment is implemented through a community-voted program upgrade. The process can only reduce, pause, or halt the mint; any increase, acceleration, restart, or other amendment requires a standard community HIP at the 66.67% supermajority.

**What the Council cannot do:**

- Unilaterally halt, pause, or amend the Supplement on-chain.
- Direct the Receiving Entity's business strategy, hiring, vendor selection, or product decisions.
- Override or set the carrier-paid payer/burn rate. Authority over the rate remains with Nova under [HIP 143][hip-143]; the Council has insight into rate-setting as it relates to the goals of the mint and may publish dissent or recommend curtailment, but does not set, override, or direct the rate.
- Pre-approve or block any individual Supplement sale or spend.
- Bind the Receiving Entity to any commitment, contract, or expenditure beyond those in the Receiving Entity Agreement or elsewhere in HIP 149.
- Speak on behalf of the Receiving Entity publicly, unless specifically authorized in writing.

## Duty of Care and Loyalty

By accepting a Council seat and signing the CPA, each member agrees to a **contractual duty of care and loyalty to the Helium Network**:

- **Good faith.** Act honestly and in good faith in all Council activities.
- **Reasonable diligence.** Review materials provided by the Receiving Entity, attend meetings, and participate meaningfully in Council deliberations.
- **Network-first obligation.** When exercising Council authority, prioritize the interests of the Helium Network as a whole over personal interests as an individual HNT holder, deployer, or business operator.
- **No self-dealing.** Do not use the Council position to obtain a personal benefit at the expense of the network or other stakeholders.

This is a contractual obligation, not a fiduciary duty. It is enforceable under the CPA and is scoped exclusively to the Council member role. Breach is grounds for removal.

## Information Access

The information rights below exist to enable the Council to perform its Supplement oversight function. They are not a general right of inspection into the Receiving Entity's operations, finances, or business relationships. "Protected Information" means information the Receiving Entity is prohibited or restricted from disclosing due to contractual, statutory, regulatory, or corporate-governance obligations.

**Pre-mint onboarding.** There is a minimum period of two (2) weeks after the Council is seated before the mint begins, during which the Council conducts initial diligence and may recommend a curtailment before minting starts. Within 48 hours of seating, the Receiving Entity turns over the materials approved for Council review; any delay extends the mint start by an equal period.

**Standing reports (quarterly):**

- **Category-level financials.** Profit-and-loss and balance-sheet data presenting total revenues, total expenses, total assets, total liabilities, and total equity accurately, with categories aggregated where necessary to avoid disclosing Protected Information. These are management-prepared oversight reports, not audited financial statements.
- **Supplement outflow report.** Categorized breakdown of Supplement vault spending (e.g., carrier expansion, deployer programs, engineering, ecosystem grants, regulatory, operating costs).
- **Headcount summary.** Aggregated, anonymized; no individual names, titles, or compensation figures.
- **Ecosystem grant summary.** Grants made from the Supplement during the quarter: recipient, HNT amount, stated purpose.
- **Carrier expansion and revenue report.** Carriers in pipeline, signed, and live, with the aggregate Supplement amount deployed toward carrier expansion during the quarter.

**Supplement sales reporting (ongoing, not limited to quarterly):** type, size, timing, and lockup terms of Supplement sales, aggregated so as not to disclose Protected Information.

**Initial reporting package:** summary financial statements for fiscal year 2026 and projected pro forma financial statements for the following fiscal year, updated on a reasonable and ongoing basis throughout the life of the mint.

**Further diligence (on request):** the Council may request additional information, including vendor-specific cost detail. The Receiving Entity provides information reasonably necessary to evaluate whether Supplement funds are used consistently with the stated goals, exclusive of Protected Information, and determines whether a request meets that standard; requests outside it are declined with a written explanation.

**What the Council will not receive:**

| Category | Reason |
| ----- | ----- |
| Protected Information | Legally or contractually restricted from disclosure |
| Per-transaction detail, the general ledger, or direct access to books or accounting systems | Beyond structured Supplement oversight |
| Tax filings | Proprietary; not relevant to the mandate |
| Individual employee compensation or salary data (department-aggregated figures are acceptable) | Legally protected private information |
| The Receiving Entity's capitalization table | Proprietary corporate information unrelated to the mandate |
| Board materials, investor communications, or equity-related documents | Outside the mandate |

Where the Council believes a declined request leaves it unable to perform its oversight role, its recourse is to escalate under the Authority section (publish dissent, recommend course-correction, or recommend a curtailment), not to compel disclosure or halt the mint unilaterally.

## Confidentiality (CPA Summary)

Every Council member must sign the CPA before being seated. Key terms:

- All information received through the Council role is confidential unless the Receiving Entity designates it as public or it becomes public through no fault of the member.
- Confidential information may not be disclosed to any third party, including other community members, deployers, or media, without the Receiving Entity's prior written consent.
- Council members may discuss confidential information with each other in the course of Council deliberations.
- The confidentiality obligation survives the end of the Council term for three (3) years.
- Breach is grounds for immediate suspension of access and removal, and may result in legal action and other available remedies.

The CPA also contains mutual indemnification for each side's own breaches, with the Receiving Entity's liability to a member capped at that member's Supplement compensation and the member's liability for confidentiality or trading-policy breaches uncapped; neither side indemnifies the other for a member's good-faith exercise of Council authority. See the [CPA][council-cpa] for the full terms.

## HNT Trading Policy

Council members may receive material non-public information ("MNPI") about the Receiving Entity's operations and the Helium Network. The policy protects both the network and individual members from allegations of insider trading or tipping. **This is not a trading ban.** Members may hold and trade HNT, subject to the following safeguards (binding through the CPA):

- **Awareness obligation.** Members may not buy, sell, or transfer HNT (or any derivative token) while in possession of MNPI received through the Council role. This is self-policed; the Receiving Entity cannot advise whether a member holds MNPI at any given time, and the member must make that determination case by case.
- **48-hour cooling-off period.** No HNT trades for 48 hours following a Council meeting, receipt of a quarterly report, or receipt of any other confidential information through the Council channel. Limit orders and liquidity-pool positions established at least 48 hours before the triggering event are excluded.
- **Pre-notification for large trades.** Before any HNT transaction exceeding, in a 24-hour period, the greater of $5,000 or 1% of the member's reported or controlled holdings, the member notifies the Receiving Entity in writing. This is notification, not approval. The Receiving Entity may adjust the dollar threshold to reflect market conditions; the 1% component is fixed.
- **Quarterly certification.** Each quarter, every member submits a brief written attestation confirming no trading on MNPI received through Council activities.

If a member trades while in possession of MNPI, the Receiving Entity will cooperate fully with any resulting regulatory investigation. These safeguards exist to create a clear record that controls were maintained; following them protects members.

## Compensation

Canonical: [HIP 149 Decision 4][hip149-d4]. The five community-nominated members share **1.25% of the Supplement** (~1.76M HNT total; ~352,500 HNT per seat over the program if all five remain seated), distributed pro-rata among the seated community members and minted directly to their wallets. The full 1.25% is always distributed: a vacant seat's share is distributed among the seated community members, not returned to the vault. The Receiving Entity is not the custodian or payer of these funds. The two Receiving-Entity-appointed seats serve unpaid.

- A member accrues compensation only while seated.
- The total allocation may be adjusted by Council vote or community vote but may not exceed 1.25% of the Supplement.
- Members receiving compensation must pass KYC and are responsible for their personal tax obligations.
- All compensation is publicly reported by the Council on a quarterly basis.
- There is no Council-controlled discretionary or operating fund. Each community-nominated member funds any independent legal counsel or professional services from their own compensation. The Receiving Entity provides its appointed members with any legal support needed in connection with their Council service.
- Self-dealing in connection with compensation is grounds for removal.

## Removal and Vacancies

A Council member may be removed **for cause**, as set forth in the CPA. "For cause" includes:

- Fraud or dishonesty in connection with Council activities.
- Violation of the duty of care and loyalty described above.
- Breach of the CPA or the trading policy.
- Sustained failure to participate in Council activities, as specified in the CPA.
- Conduct that materially harms the Helium Network.

For-cause removal from a seat is a governance matter: a Council vote (quorum 5 of 7, including at least 3 community seats; simple majority) followed by a community vote. Separately, the Receiving Entity may immediately suspend a member's access to confidential information and MNPI where it determines the member has breached the CPA, the trading policy, or the confidentiality obligations, or engaged in fraud; this protects the information shared and is not subject to Council override. Suspension does not by itself vacate the seat, but a suspended member cannot perform the oversight role and the seat is treated as vacant for replacement.

A member may also be removed **without cause** by a simple-majority vote consisting of at least four (4) votes to remove, regardless of quorum.

**Vacancies.** When one or more community-nominated seats are vacant for any reason, the mint continues and the Council retains full authority, including escalation. Quorum adjusts to the seated members minus one, provided at least two community-nominated members remain seated. If fewer than two community-nominated members are seated, the Council cannot act and the mint pauses until at least three community-nominated members are seated. An expedited replacement vote, run like the initial seating, is initiated within 14 days of the vacancy and completed within 30 days. The Receiving Entity's two appointed seats may be replaced by the Receiving Entity at any time at its discretion.

## Relationship to the CPA

The CPA is the binding contract between the Receiving Entity and the individual member; its terms are set by the Receiving Entity. This Governance Framework is community-owned governance established under HIP 149. The CPA references this document for context only; it does not incorporate this document as binding terms, and a later community amendment to this document does not by itself change a signed CPA. The CPA governs how shared information is handled and the member's personal obligations; it does not alter the Council's authority under HIP 149, which is established by community governance and enforceable through on-chain mechanisms independent of the Receiving Entity. Where this document and a signed CPA differ as to a binding member obligation, the CPA as executed governs for that member.
