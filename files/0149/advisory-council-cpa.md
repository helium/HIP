# Advisory Council: Confidentiality and Participation Agreement (CPA)

*Prepared by Nova Labs, Inc. for prospective HIP-149 Advisory Council members. Draft for review.*

**Status: draft.** This is the binding agreement each Advisory Council member signs with Nova Labs, Inc. ("Nova") before being seated. It is published here for community transparency. It is not itself part of the HIP 149 vote and is not created or amended through a HIP. Member obligations and Nova's commitments take effect through this agreement; the [Advisory Council: Roles, Responsibilities, and Access][council-roles] document (the "Governance Framework") is referenced for context and does not independently create obligations enforceable by either party against the other.

---

This Confidentiality and Participation Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between: **Nova Labs, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation ("Nova"), and **[Member Name]**, a resident of **[jurisdiction]** ("Member"), each a "Party" and together the "Parties."

## Section 1: Background and Purpose

1.1 Member has been selected to serve on the Advisory Council (the "**Council**") established under the Helium Improvement Proposal designated HIP-149, as approved by the Helium community (the "**HIP**").

1.2 The purpose of the Council is to support the Helium Network by overseeing the Supplement allocation and monitoring progress toward the stated goals of the HIP: (a) long-term financial viability of the entity managing the Helium Network, with the target of becoming cash-flow neutral within the three-year mint period; and (b) continued expansion of the Helium Network with increased HNT burn in a financially sustainable manner (collectively, the "**Stated Goals**").

1.3 This Agreement sets out the Member's obligations and Nova's commitments in connection with the Member's service on the Council.

1.4 Member may not be seated on the Council until this Agreement is fully executed by both Parties.

1.5 Nova is the entity receiving the Supplement (the "Receiving Entity") as that term is defined in HIP-149 and the Governance Framework. References in HIP-149 and the Governance Framework to the Receiving Entity mean Nova for so long as Nova holds that role.

## Section 2: Defined Terms

As used in this Agreement, the following terms have the meanings set forth below. Capitalized terms used but not defined in this Section have the meanings assigned to them elsewhere in this Agreement.

2.1 "**Confidential Information**" means all non-public information received by the Member through the Member's Council role, including but not limited to financial data, business plans, carrier relationships, pricing, operational metrics, and strategic plans, and any other information designated as confidential by Nova. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) becomes publicly available through no fault of the Member; (b) the Member possessed before receiving it through the Council, as demonstrated by contemporaneous written records; (c) the Member independently developed without use of or reference to Confidential Information, as demonstrated by contemporaneous written records; or (d) the Member received from a third party who was not, to the Member's knowledge, under a confidentiality obligation to Nova with respect to such information.

2.2 "**Council**" has the meaning set forth in Section 1.1.

2.3 "**Governance Framework**" means the document titled "Advisory Council: Roles, Responsibilities, and Access" appended to the HIP, which establishes the Council's governance procedures, authority, and mandate. The Governance Framework is referenced in this Agreement for context only and does not independently create obligations enforceable by either Party against the other.

2.4 "**HIP**" has the meaning set forth in Section 1.1.

2.5 "**MNPI**" means material non-public information regarding Nova's operations or the Helium Network that the Member receives through the Member's Council role.

2.6 "**Protected Information**" means Confidential Information that Nova is prohibited or restricted from disclosing due to contractual obligations to third parties, applicable statutes, regulatory requirements, or corporate governance obligations, including but not limited to tax filings, individual compensation data, cap table information, board materials, investor communications, equity-related documents, per-transaction detail, and general ledger records.

2.7 "**Stated Goals**" has the meaning set forth in Section 1.2.

2.8 "**Supplement**" means the HNT mint allocation authorized by HIP-149 Decision 2, consisting of a total allocation of 141,000,000 HNT minted per-epoch into a vault, across two periods: (a) the **First Window**, a period of approximately twelve (12) months at a fixed mint rate of approximately 196,000 HNT per epoch; followed by (b) the **Second Window**, a period of approximately twenty-four (24) months during which the per-epoch mint rate decays linearly from approximately 196,000 HNT to zero. Both windows are encoded on-chain at passage with fixed boundary timestamps. No future vote is required to end them; they self-terminate.

## Section 3: Relationship to the Governance Framework

3.1 The Council's mandate and governance procedures are established by the HIP and the Governance Framework. The Council's authority under the Governance Framework is limited to the oversight functions described in this Agreement and does not extend to any authority over Nova's operations, management, or business decisions.

3.2 This Agreement is the sole and complete agreement between Nova and the Member regarding the Member's service on the Council, as further set forth in Section 12.3. The Governance Framework does not create, expand, or modify any obligation of either Party under this Agreement.

3.3 If the Governance Framework is amended by community vote, such amendment does not alter the rights or obligations of either Party under this Agreement. Any change to the Parties' rights or obligations requires a written amendment to this Agreement signed by both Parties in accordance with Section 12.4.

3.4 The Governance Framework does not confer any rights on the Member enforceable against Nova beyond what is expressly stated in this Agreement.

## Section 4: Relationship of the Parties

4.1 Nothing in this Agreement creates a corporate, fiduciary, agency, partnership, employment, joint venture, or trustee relationship between Nova and the Member. The Member is not an employee, agent, or representative of Nova for any purpose.

4.2 The Member may not speak on behalf of Nova publicly or to any third party unless specifically authorized in writing by Nova in advance.

4.3 The Member may not bind Nova to any commitment, contract, expenditure, or obligation of any kind.

4.4 The Member's identity will be disclosed to the Helium community in connection with the Member's Council service. The Member may not serve on the Council anonymously.

## Section 5: Council Member Obligations

### 5.1 Duty of Care and Loyalty

5.1.1 The Member will participate in Council activities in good faith and with reasonable diligence, including reviewing materials provided by Nova, attending scheduled meetings, and participating meaningfully in Council deliberations.

5.1.2 When exercising any authority granted to the Council under this Agreement, the Member will act in the best interests of the Helium Network as a whole, without preference for the Member's personal interests, any particular stakeholder group, or any third party.

5.1.3 The Member will not engage in self-dealing in connection with the Member's Council role. For purposes of this Agreement, "self-dealing" means using the Member's Council position, access to Confidential Information, or influence over Council decisions to obtain a personal benefit not available to the Helium community generally, other than the compensation described in Section 10.

5.1.4 The obligations in this Section 5.1 are contractual only. They do not create fiduciary, agency, partnership, trustee, or other duties of any kind, whether at law or in equity, and are scoped exclusively to the Member's Council role. They do not apply to the Member's activities outside of Council service.

5.1.5 Breach of this Section 5.1 is grounds for removal in accordance with Section 9.

### 5.2 Confidentiality

5.2.1 All information received by the Member through the Member's Council role is Confidential Information unless Nova designates it as public or it falls within the exclusions set forth in Section 2.1.

5.2.2 The Member will not disclose Confidential Information to any third party, including other Helium community members, deployers, media, or any person or entity not serving on the Council, without Nova's prior written consent.

5.2.3 Notwithstanding Section 5.2.2, Members may discuss Confidential Information with one another in the course of Council deliberations.

5.2.4 The Member will use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the Member's Council duties and for no other purpose.

5.2.5 The Member will use reasonable measures to protect Confidential Information from unauthorized disclosure, including securing any documents, files, or communications containing Confidential Information.

5.2.6 Upon the Member's removal, resignation, or the end of the Member's Council term, the Member will promptly return to Nova or destroy all Confidential Information in the Member's possession, custody, or control, and will certify in writing that the Member has done so. This obligation does not require the Member to return or destroy Confidential Information that the Member is required to retain by applicable law, provided that any such retained information remains subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

5.2.7 The obligations in this Section 5.2 survive the end of the Member's Council term for a period of three (3) years.

5.2.8 Breach of this Section 5.2 is grounds for removal in accordance with Section 9 and may result in legal action by Nova, including a claim for injunctive relief.

### 5.3 HNT Trading Policy

5.3.1 The Member may hold and trade HNT. This Section 5.3 does not impose a general trading ban. The following provisions are safeguards designed to protect the integrity of the Council and the Helium Network.

5.3.2 **Awareness Obligation.** The Member will not execute any trade in HNT while the Member is in possession of MNPI received through the Member's Council role. Compliance with this obligation is self-policed by the Member.

5.3.3 **Cooling-Off Period.** The Member will not execute any trade in HNT during the forty-eight (48) hours following: (a) any Council meeting; (b) receipt of a quarterly report under Section 6.1; or (c) receipt of any other Confidential Information through the Council channel.

5.3.4 **Pre-Existing Orders and Positions.** Section 5.3.3 does not apply to limit orders or liquidity pool positions that were established at least forty-eight (48) hours before the event triggering the cooling-off period.

5.3.5 **Pre-Notification of Large Trades.** Before executing any trade or series of related trades in HNT that exceeds, in a twenty-four (24) hour period, the greater of: (a) $5,000 (in USD or USD equivalent); or (b) one percent (1%) of the Member's reported or controlled HNT holdings, the Member will provide prior written notice to Nova. This is a notification requirement only; Nova's approval is not required. Nova may adjust the dollar threshold in clause (a) upon written notice to the Member and the Council. The percentage threshold in clause (b) is fixed for the term of this Agreement.

5.3.6 **Quarterly Certification.** The Member will provide Nova with a written certification on a quarterly basis confirming the Member's compliance with this Section 5.3 during the preceding quarter.

5.3.7 **Cooperation with Investigations.** If the Member executes a trade while in possession of MNPI, Nova will cooperate fully with any resulting regulatory investigation. The Member will likewise cooperate with any such investigation and will not obstruct or impede any inquiry by a governmental authority.

5.3.8 Breach of this Section 5.3 is grounds for removal in accordance with Section 9 and may result in legal action by Nova.

### 5.4 Participation Requirements

5.4.1 Sustained failure to participate in Council activities is grounds for removal under Section 9.2. Unless modified by the Council pursuant to Section 5.4.2, "sustained failure to participate" means any of the following: (a) failure to attend three (3) or more consecutive scheduled Council meetings without prior notice to the Council; (b) failure to attend at least sixty percent (60%) of all scheduled Council meetings in any rolling six-month period; (c) failure to respond to Council communications requiring a response within seven (7) calendar days on two (2) or more occasions in any rolling three-month period; or (d) failure to cast a vote in two (2) or more consecutive Council votes without prior notice to the Council.

5.4.2 The Council may, by Council vote, modify the thresholds set forth in Section 5.4.1(a) through (d) or adopt additional participation standards. Any such modifications and standards must be communicated to all Members in writing and shall take effect upon such notice.

## Section 6: Nova Obligations

### 6.1 Information Access

6.1.1 **Scope.** The information rights in this Section 6 exist to enable the Council to perform its oversight function with respect to the Supplement and the Stated Goals. They are not a general right of inspection into Nova's operations, finances, or business relationships. Being seated on the Council does not entitle the Member to open-ended access to Nova's finances, operations, or corporate records. The Council has structured visibility into the Supplement and the Stated Goals as defined by this Agreement.

6.1.2 **Pre-Mint Onboarding.**

(a) There will be a minimum period of two (2) weeks after the Council is seated before the mint begins, during which the Council may conduct initial diligence and, if the Council determines it necessary, pause or delay the commencement of the mint.

(b) Within forty-eight (48) hours of a Member's seating, Nova will provide initial materials for Council review. Any delay by Nova in providing those materials extends the mint start date by a period equal to the delay.

6.1.3 **Standing Quarterly Reports.** During the term of this Agreement, Nova will provide the Council with quarterly reports containing the following:

(a) **Financial Summary.** Reasonably detailed category-level profit-and-loss and balance sheets, including total revenues, total expenses, total assets, total liabilities, and total equity. Financial reports are management-prepared oversight reports, not audited financial statements. Categories may be aggregated where necessary to avoid disclosure of Protected Information.

(b) **Supplement Outflow Report.** A categorized breakdown of Supplement vault expenditures, including: deployer programs, engineering, ecosystem grants, regulatory costs, and operating costs.

(c) **Headcount Summary.** An aggregated, anonymized summary of Nova's headcount.

(d) **Ecosystem Grant Summary.** A summary of each ecosystem grant disbursed during the quarter, including the recipient, HNT amount, and stated purpose.

(e) **Carrier Expansion and Revenue Report.** A report covering carriers in pipeline, signed, and live, and the aggregate Supplement amount deployed toward carrier expansion.

6.1.4 **Supplement Sales Reporting.** On an ongoing basis and not limited to the quarterly reporting cycle, Nova will report all Supplement sales activity to the Council, including the type, size, timing, and lockup terms of each sale. Sales reports may be aggregated in a manner that does not disclose Protected Information.

6.1.5 **Initial Reporting Package.** Nova will provide the Council with: (a) summary financial statements for fiscal year 2026; and (b) projected pro forma financial statements for the following fiscal year, each updated on a reasonable and ongoing basis throughout the life of the mint.

6.1.6 **General.**

(a) Reports provided under this Section 6.1 will not include Protected Information.

(b) The Council may request additional information beyond the standing reports described in this Section 6.1, including vendor-specific cost detail. Nova will provide, exclusive of Protected Information, information that is reasonably necessary to evaluate whether Supplement funds are being used consistently with the Stated Goals. Nova determines, in its reasonable judgment, whether a request meets that standard. Requests that Nova determines do not meet that standard will be declined with a written explanation.

### 6.2 Recourse for Declined, Incomplete, or Inaccurate Information

6.2.1 If the Council believes that a declined request, or information that is incomplete or inaccurate, leaves it unable to perform its oversight role, the Council's recourse is limited to the following, in sequence:

(a) **Publish Dissent.** The Council may publish a statement of dissent regarding the information gap.

(b) **Recommend Course-Correction.** The Council may deliver a written recommendation to Nova specifying the corrective action it believes is necessary.

(c) **Recommend Curtailment.** The Council may recommend a curtailment of the Supplement (a reduction, pause, or halt of the mint) in accordance with the process set forth in Section 8.4.2.

6.2.2 The Council may not compel disclosure, compel correction, halt the mint unilaterally, or pursue any other remedy against Nova for failure to disclose, incomplete disclosure, or inaccurate disclosure.

### 6.3 Information Nova Will Not Provide

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, Nova shall not and is not obligated to provide the Council with Protected Information or direct access to Nova's books, records, or systems.

### 6.4 Confidentiality of Member Information

Nova will not disclose the Member's trading notifications submitted under Section 5.3.5, the Member's positions taken during Council deliberations, or other member-specific information to any person or entity outside of Council operations, except: (a) as required by applicable law or regulation; (b) in connection with a regulatory investigation; or (c) in connection with the enforcement of this Agreement.

## Section 7: Supplement Oversight and No Rights in Unminted Tokens

### 7.1 Oversight Scope

7.1.1 The Council oversees the allocation and use of the Supplement to monitor whether funds are being used effectively to advance the Stated Goals.

7.1.2 The Council's oversight authority is limited to matters materially related to the Supplement, including its use, allocation, monetization, DC burn alignment, market impact, and progress toward the Stated Goals.

7.1.3 The Council's oversight role does not create any ownership, entitlement, or property interest in the Supplement, any portion thereof, or any proceeds derived therefrom.

### 7.2 Limitations on Council Authority

7.2.1 The Council may not direct Nova's business strategy, hiring, vendor selection, or product decisions.

7.2.2 The Council may not override or set the carrier-paid payer/burn rate. Authority over the payer/burn rate remains with Nova under HIP-143. The Council has insight into rate-setting to the extent it relates to the Stated Goals and may publish dissent or recommend curtailment in accordance with Section 6.2, but the Council does not set, override, or direct the rate.

7.2.3 The Council does not pre-approve or block individual Supplement sales. The Council oversees conformance with the monetization guidelines described in Section 8.4.1(c) and may publish dissent, recommend course-correction, or recommend curtailment in accordance with Section 6.2 if it believes Nova is not acting in good faith.

### 7.3 No Rights in Unminted or Unallocated Tokens

7.3.1 No person or entity, including Nova, the Member, any other Council member, or any contributor, advisor, participant, or affiliate of any of the foregoing, has any right, title, interest, entitlement, expectancy, or claim of any kind in or to any portion of the Supplement that has not been both minted and transferred to a wallet designated for such person or entity pursuant to an authorized allocation. Tokens are deemed owned by a person or entity only upon actual transfer to that person's or entity's designated wallet address.

7.3.2 For the Supplement authorized under Decision 2 of the HIP, the on-chain vault whose address is fixed at the program upgrade is the designated receiving wallet, and each per-epoch mint is transferred to that vault upon minting. No separate allocation process, custodian, or third-party authorization stands between the mint and that vault.

7.3.3 No prior communication, course of dealing, Council membership, participation, expectation, allocation discussion, projection, or other statement creates any right or entitlement to tokens unless such tokens have been actually transferred to the applicable designated wallet pursuant to an authorized allocation.

7.3.4 To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, the Member irrevocably waives, releases, and covenants not to assert any claim, demand, action, cause of action, or proceeding, whether at law or in equity, arising out of or relating to any alleged right, expectancy, entitlement, or ownership interest in any unminted tokens or any minted tokens not transferred to the Member's designated wallet.

7.3.5 Acceptance, claiming, receipt, transfer, sale, staking, voting, use, or other exercise of control over any allocated minted tokens constitutes the accepting party's agreement to and acceptance of this Section 7.3.

## Section 8: Indemnification and Limitation of Remedies

### 8.1 Mutual Indemnification

8.1.1 **By the Member.** The Member will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Nova and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to the Member's breach of this Agreement, including the confidentiality obligations in Section 5.2, the trading policy in Section 5.3, or the duty of care and loyalty in Section 5.1.

8.1.2 **By Nova.** Nova will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Member from and against any third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to Nova's breach of its obligations under this Agreement, including its disclosure obligations under Section 6 and its confidentiality obligations under Section 6.4.

### 8.2 Liability Caps

8.2.1 Nova's aggregate liability to the Member under this Agreement, whether in contract, tort, or otherwise, is capped at an amount equal to the total number of HNT actually received by the Member as Supplement compensation under Section 10, measured in HNT (not USD or any other currency equivalent).

8.2.2 The Member's aggregate liability to Nova for breaches of the confidentiality obligations in Section 5.2 or the trading policy in Section 5.3 is uncapped.

8.2.3 The Member's aggregate liability to Nova for all other breaches of this Agreement is capped at an amount equal to the total number of HNT actually received by the Member as Supplement compensation under Section 10, measured in HNT (not USD or any other currency equivalent).

8.2.4 For purposes of any arbitration award or judgment requiring conversion of the caps in Sections 8.2.1 or 8.2.3 to USD, the applicable HNT amount will be converted at the volume-weighted average price of HNT on CoinGecko (or, if CoinGecko is unavailable, a comparable publicly available data aggregator selected by the arbitrator) for the thirty (30) calendar days preceding the date of the award.

### 8.3 Exclusions

8.3.1 Neither Party is obligated to indemnify the other for any claim arising out of the Member's good-faith exercise of Council authority or participation in Council governance activities, including voting, publishing dissent, recommending course-correction, or recommending curtailment under Section 6.2.

### 8.4 Limitation of Remedies

8.4.1 The Member acknowledges and agrees that the Council's sole recourse for Nova's failure to honor commitments related to the Supplement, including but not limited to any of the following, is to recommend a curtailment of the Supplement in accordance with Section 8.4.2:

(a) **Reporting Failures.** Failure to provide standing reports, sales reports, or other information required under Section 6.

(b) **Incomplete or Inaccurate Information.** Provision of information that is incomplete or inaccurate.

(c) **Monetization Guidelines.** Failure to comply with the following monetization guidelines: preference for OTC sales over open-market or exchange sales of the Supplement allocation, conducted in good faith and to the extent commercially practicable; and imposition of reasonable lockup periods on OTC purchasers so as not to flood the market before the long-term benefits of the allocation are realized.

(d) **Non-Compete.** Failure to comply with the following restriction: Nova will not fund, develop, or acquire any blockchain network or token that competes with the Helium Network or HNT during the Supplement period and for three (3) years thereafter. This restriction does not prevent ordinary business activity, integrations, partnerships, software development, or technology work that primarily supports the Helium Network, increases adoption, improves utility, increases DC burn, supports deployer economics, or contributes to long-term sustainability.

(e) **DC Burn Alignment.** Failure to align DC burn with carrier offload revenue. Nova commits, to the greatest extent commercially practicable, to process carrier offload usage fees through the DC burn mechanism, acknowledging that offload revenue cannot always be determined precisely at the time of processing and/or that periodic reconciliation may be required. Authority over the payer/burn rate remains with Nova under HIP-143.

(f) **Long-Term Alignment.** Failure to pursue long-term alignment in good faith. Nova will work in good faith to align its interests with those of token holders over the life of the mint and thereafter, to the extent consistent with Nova's obligations to its equity holders and applicable law. Nova will present recommendations to the Council and community on how that alignment could be encoded in the mint-and-burn protocol, with an initial recommendation within six (6) months of mint start. Any such encoding is implemented through the community's normal governance process (HIP and program upgrade), not by Nova or Council directive.

8.4.2 **Curtailment Process.** A recommendation to curtail the Supplement (whether a reduction, pause, or halt of the mint) requires:

(a) a Council vote, with a quorum of five (5) of seven (7) seated members including at least three (3) community-nominated seats, approved by simple majority, following a five (5) day voting period;

(b) following approval by the Council, a community vote, announced within three (3) days of the Council vote, with a minimum seven (7) day discussion period and a seven (7) day voting period, approved by a simple majority of voted veHNT and subject to the quorum required by the HIP; and

(c) on community approval, implementation through a community-voted program upgrade.

8.4.3 The curtailment process may only reduce, pause, or halt the mint. Any increase, acceleration, or restart of the mint requires a standard community HIP approved at supermajority.

8.4.4 Neither the Member nor the Council may compel performance, seek damages, or pursue any other remedy against Nova for non-compliance with the commitments described in Section 8.4.1.

8.4.5 The limitations in this Section 8.4 do not affect either Party's rights under Section 8.1 for breach of the confidentiality obligations in Section 5.2, the trading policy in Section 5.3, or Nova's disclosure obligations under Section 6.4.

## Section 9: Removal and Suspension

### 9.1 For-Cause Suspension of Information Access

9.1.1 Nova may immediately suspend and terminate a Member's access to Confidential Information where Nova determines, in its sole discretion, that the Member has breached this Agreement or engaged in fraud.

9.1.2 A suspension under this Section 9.1 is not subject to Council override.

9.1.3 Suspension of information access under this Section 9.1 does not remove the Member from the Member's Council seat. Removal requires the governance process set forth in Section 9.2 or 9.3. However, a Member whose information access has been suspended is unable to perform the Council's oversight function and will not be counted toward quorum or participate in Council votes. The vacancy procedures in Section 9.4 apply, and the Council will initiate replacement proceedings promptly.

### 9.2 For-Cause Removal from Seat

9.2.1 Removal of a Member from the Member's Council seat is a governance matter handled by the Council and the community.

9.2.2 A Member may be removed for cause by a Council vote, with a quorum of five (5) of seven (7) seated members including at least three (3) community-nominated seats, approved by simple majority, followed by a community vote.

9.2.3 Grounds for removal for cause are:

(a) fraud or dishonesty in connection with Council activities;

(b) violation of the duty of care and loyalty under Section 5.1;

(c) breach of the confidentiality obligations under Section 5.2 or the trading policy under Section 5.3;

(d) sustained failure to participate in Council activities as defined in Section 5.4;

(e) conduct that materially harms the Helium Network; or

(f) illegal activity in connection with the Member's Council service or Supplement compensation.

### 9.3 Without-Cause Removal

A Member may be removed without cause by a simple majority vote consisting of at least four (4) votes to remove, regardless of quorum.

### 9.4 Replacement

9.4.1 Replacement of a community-nominated Member will be handled by a community vote consistent with the initial seating process.

9.4.2 Nova may replace its appointed Members at any time in its discretion.

9.4.3 When one or more community-nominated seats are simultaneously vacant for any reason, the following applies:

(a) The mint continues without pause or interruption.

(b) The Council retains full authority, including the ability to recommend curtailment under Section 8.4.

(c) Quorum adjusts proportionally to the number of seated members minus one, provided at least two (2) community-nominated members remain seated.

(d) If fewer than two (2) community-nominated members remain seated, the Council cannot act until replacements are seated, and the mint pauses until at least three (3) community-nominated members are seated.

(e) An expedited replacement vote must be initiated within fourteen (14) days of the vacancy and completed within thirty (30) days.

(f) Nova will cooperate in facilitating the replacement process.

## Section 10: Compensation

### 10.1 Community-Nominated Members

10.1.1 The 1.25% of the Supplement allocated to community-nominated compensation is distributed pro-rata among the seated community-nominated Council members and minted directly to their designated wallet addresses. The full allocation is always distributed: the share of a vacant community-nominated seat is distributed among the seated community-nominated members rather than withheld. Nova is not the custodian, intermediary, or payer of these funds.

10.1.2 Members are compensated only while seated on the Council. A Member who is removed, resigns, or whose information access is suspended under Section 9.1 ceases to accrue compensation as of the effective date of such event.

10.1.3 The total compensation allocation may be adjusted by Council vote or community vote, but may not exceed 1.25% of the Supplement under any circumstances.

### 10.2 Guardrails

10.2.1 Any Member receiving compensation under this Section 10 must satisfy know-your-customer ("KYC") verification requirements as determined by Nova prior to receiving any tokens.

10.2.2 Each Member is individually responsible for all tax obligations arising from compensation received under this Agreement. Nova will not withhold taxes, issue tax forms, or provide tax advice in connection with Supplement compensation. The Member will indemnify Nova for any tax liability, penalty, or assessment imposed on Nova as a result of the Member's failure to satisfy the Member's personal tax obligations. The Member's indemnification obligation under this Section 10.2.2 is not subject to the liability caps in Section 8.2.

10.2.3 All compensation paid to Council members will be publicly reported by the Council on a quarterly basis.

10.2.4 Self-dealing in connection with compensation is grounds for removal under Section 9.2.

### 10.3 Nova-Appointed Members

10.3.1 Nova's appointed Council members serve without compensation from the Supplement.

10.3.2 Nova is responsible for providing its appointed members with any legal support needed in connection with their Council service.

10.3.3 Nova is responsible for the conduct of its appointed members in connection with Council activities. Any breach of this Agreement by a Nova-appointed member is attributable to Nova for purposes of Section 8.1.

### 10.4 Independent Legal Counsel

Each community-nominated Member is individually responsible for obtaining any independent legal counsel or professional services the Member requires in connection with the Member's Council role, at the Member's own expense and from the Member's Supplement compensation.

## Section 11: Term and Termination

### 11.1 Effective Date

This Agreement is effective upon execution by both Parties.

### 11.2 Termination

This Agreement terminates when the Member's Council service ends, which occurs upon the earliest of:

(a) a community vote to dissolve the Council;

(b) full distribution of the Supplement (141,000,000 HNT);

(c) Nova's written notice that it declines further distributions from the Supplement;

(d) the Member's removal from the Council under Section 9.2 or 9.3; or

(e) the Member's voluntary resignation from the Council, effective upon written notice to Nova and the Council.

### 11.3 Early Suspension of the Supplement

11.3.1 If the Supplement is suspended before full distribution through a curtailment approved under Section 8.4.2, this Agreement and the Council will survive for a period of ninety (90) days following the effective date of the suspension to facilitate restart discussions.

11.3.2 At the end of the ninety (90) day period, this Agreement terminates automatically unless a HIP to restart the Supplement is actively in the voting process at that time, in which case this Agreement continues until the earlier of: (a) the conclusion of the vote; or (b) thirty (30) days after the end of the initial ninety (90) day period.

11.3.3 If the restart HIP is approved, this Agreement continues in full force and effect. If the restart HIP fails, this Agreement terminates upon conclusion of the vote.

### 11.4 Surviving Obligations

The following provisions survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement:

(a) **Confidentiality** (Section 5.2): three (3) years following termination;

(b) **HNT Trading Policy** (Section 5.3): for so long as the Member remains in possession of MNPI received through the Member's Council role;

(c) **Mutual Indemnification** (Section 8.1);

(d) **Liability Caps** (Section 8.2);

(e) **No Rights in Unminted or Unallocated Tokens** (Section 7.3);

(f) **Limitation of Remedies** (Section 8.4);

(g) **Confidentiality of Member Information** (Section 6.4): co-terminus with the Member's confidentiality obligations under clause (a) above;

(h) **Tax Indemnity** (Section 10.2.2); and
